Are you passionate about Earth observation? Eager to explore cutting-edge techniques in self-supervised learning? Look no further! Our specialized course will equip you with an overview of existing SSL techniques before we discuss the specific characteristics of Earth observation data that provide both challenges and opportunities in SSL.

Course Overview

  • Dates: December 16 - 20, 2024
  • Location: Indian Institute of Remote Sensing, 4, Kalidas Rd, Hathibarkala, Dehradun, Uttarakhand 248001, India

Why Attend?

  • Get an overview of self-supervised learning (SSL) methods.
  • Discuss challenges and opportunities for SSL tailored for Earth observation data.
  • Collaborate with fellow PhD students and build a strong network within the community.
  • Gain hands-on experience through practical projects.

Who Should Attend?

  • PhD students in computer science, remote sensing, geospatial sciences, or related fields.
  • Researchers and practitioners interested in leveraging self-supervised learning for Earth observation tasks.


Course Requirements

  • Knowledge of basic Deep Learning concepts (e.g., supervised learning).
  • Experience with Pytorch (highly recommended).
